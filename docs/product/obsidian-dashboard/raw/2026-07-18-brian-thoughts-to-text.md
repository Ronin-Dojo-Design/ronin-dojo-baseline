## Summary (operator's cleaned version)

Learning from a YouTube video, the Obsidian note-taking process involves a step-by-step
approach. First, watch the video. Next, use Gemini to summarize it, and then create an action
plan or to-do list from the summary. This process can be automated to convert notes into
Markdown files and send them directly to a queue or ledger for cloud storage.

Discussing this workflow with Petey, we aim to integrate it with the Obsidian Vault admin
dashboard. The dashboard should mirror the Obsidian Vault's functionality, ensuring offline
access to the site, CRM content, and posting strategies. Tracking what works and what doesn't,
we can scrape web information from sources like Reddit, YouTube, and podcasts to stay current
with trends in martial arts.

For branding, the app should feel as comfortable as a gi, with subtle stitching and design
elements that evoke a sense of tradition and wear. The branding should connect users to the
martial arts community, providing a sense of security and belonging. The website should have a
bow-in and bow-out feature, mimicking the traditional martial arts greeting.

The Obsidian dashboard can serve as a skin playground, with designs inspired by tatami mats,
wood, and bamboo. Incorporating sponsorships from brands like Fuji mats and BJJ Fanatics can
enhance the user experience and provide exclusive benefits for members.

## Structured transcription

Obsidian Note Process (YouTube-based workflow):

- Step 1: Find and watch video
- Step 2: Use Gemini to summarize video
- Step 3: Summarize again into an action plan / to-do list
- Step 4: Optional shortcut automation: if-then step to convert summary to Markdown;
  automatically send Markdown file into queue/ledger in cloud or repo; result: notes
  immediately available in repository/Obsidian vault

- Discuss with PD possibility of automation workflow aligning with Obsidian vault
