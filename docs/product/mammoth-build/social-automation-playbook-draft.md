# The Mammoth Social System

**Built all the way through — and seen all the way through.**

Mammoth's promise is that a project is never quoted, shipped, and dropped. The social system
exists to make that promise *visible*: every build Mammoth carries to a satisfied installation
becomes public proof that pulls the next customer in. This is not "posting for the algorithm."
It is turning work Mammoth already does — job photos, finished buildings, happy customers — into
a lead engine that runs without adding to Michael's day.

## Why this works (the short version)

- Nearly every building buyer starts on Google, and the map results + reviews decide who gets
  the call. Mammoth's Google Business Profile and review base are therefore the single
  highest-return "social" surface — ahead of any feed.
- Buyers of shops, barns, barndominiums, and ag buildings live on Facebook and Instagram, and
  the content they respond to is exactly what Mammoth produces every week: real builds, real
  places, real transformations.
- The biggest name in this category (Morton Buildings) built its reputation on finished-building
  tours and local project proof. Mammoth can run the same play at regional scale — with a
  personality Morton doesn't have.

## The three engines

### 1. The proof engine — job photos become posts

The crew already documents builds (before / during / after). The system adds one small habit and
Ronin Building Design does the rest:

| Step | Who | What |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capture | Mammoth crew | A simple shot list per stage: site → slab → frame day → sheeting → finished wide shot. Phone photos, filed to the project like they already are. One timelapse setup on frame-up day for flagship builds. |
| Curate | RBD | Weekly: pick the best shots, pair before/afters, cut one timelapse reel per finished build. |
| Brand | RBD | Mammoth look and voice on every asset — building size, type, and town, the way the project gallery already reads ("60×84×20 Agricultural — Spokane, WA"). |
| Publish | RBD | Scheduled in weekly batches to Facebook, Instagram, and the Google profile; finished-build videos archived on a Mammoth YouTube channel. |
| Approve | Michael | One short weekly review — thumbs-up the batch, add any story or context worth telling. ~15 minutes. |

### 2. The review engine — satisfied customers say so in public

Mammoth's own completion standard — the customer confirms a satisfied installation — is the
perfect moment to ask for a review, because the ask lands right when the customer is happiest.

- When a project reaches **Satisfied Installation**, the customer receives a short, personal
  text and email (with their prior consent, captured at intake) with a one-tap link to leave a
  Google review.
- Every review gets a response in Mammoth's voice — buyers notice businesses that answer.
- The best lines from real reviews become social posts of their own, closing the loop:
  finished build → happy customer → public proof → next inquiry.

### 3. The measurement engine — leads, not likes

Because inquiries land in Mammoth's CRM with a source attached, this system is measured the way
a building business should measure it:

- Inquiries by source (Google profile, Facebook/Instagram, website, purchased lists)
- Review count and rating trend, month over month
- **Cost per customer by channel** — how social-sourced customers compare to purchased leads

No vanity metrics. If a channel doesn't produce inquiries that become projects, it gets changed.

## Platform lineup (proposed)

| Surface | Role | Cadence target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Google Business Profile | The front door: reviews, photos, service areas, weekly updates | 1–2 posts + a few fresh job photos each week |
| Facebook | Local reach + community: project posts, finished-build features | ~3 posts/week |
| Instagram | The visual portfolio: before/afters, reels, crew moments | ~3 posts/week incl. 1 reel |
| YouTube | The permanent archive: timelapses and finished-building tours | ~1 video/month, from flagship builds |
| LinkedIn / TikTok | Held for later — LinkedIn when the commercial lane warrants it; TikTok if reels prove out | Deferred |

## The 4-week starter cadence (template — slots, not commitments)

Repeats monthly; every slot filled from the proof engine.

| Week | Facebook / Instagram | Google Business Profile |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Finished-build feature** — [PROJECT: size · type · town] hero shots · **Before/after pair** — [PROJECT] · **Crew/process shot** — [STAGE] | Post: finished-build feature · photos: [3–5 job shots] |
| 2 | **Timelapse or progress reel** — [PROJECT] · **Planning tip** — from Mammoth's resource hub ([TOPIC]) · **Build-type spotlight** — [TYPE: shop / ag / barndo / arena] | Post: seasonal or planning tip · photos: [3–5 job shots] |
| 3 | **Before/after pair** — [PROJECT] · **Customer story / review quote** — [REVIEW EXCERPT] · **Frame-day / crane shot** — [PROJECT] | Post: review-quote card · photos: [3–5 job shots] |
| 4 | **Finished-build tour clip** — [PROJECT] · **"Why Mammoth" voice post** — Michael's take on [TOPIC] · **Service-area shout** — [TOWN/REGION] build | Post: build-type spotlight · photos: [3–5 job shots] |

Monthly extras: 1 YouTube upload (timelapse or tour) · review responses ongoing · one
"what to know before you build" post drawn from the existing knowledge hub.
