Boxing Combination Notes:
- Basic entries:
  - Double jab: one, one
  - One-two combinations: one, two; repeated sequences of one, two
  - Three (rear hand) and three-three: three, three (low hook, high hook)
  - Sequence examples:
    - one, one, one, two, one, two, one, two...
    - one, two, three
    - one, two, three, three
    - one, two, one, two, three, three

- Full combination described:
  - Start with basic entry: double jab (one, one)
  - Follow with repeated one-two patterns (one, two, one, two, ...)
  - Finish with three, three (low hook then high hook)

- Variations and movement:
  - Jab step outs: step out to the right or left after jabs
  - Back step: used after three, three or double jab to exit to outside/left
  - Footwork and body movement to add when entering:
    - bob
    - dip
    - stomp
    - step
    - twist
    - pivot

- Feints and timing:
  - Use double jab to "pepper" (pop, pop)
  - Use jab step as a fake to create openings
  - Hit with a feint and follow rhythm ("rock") to induce a bite
  - After opponent bites, follow with any strike:
    - back hand
    - front hand
    - left hand
    - hook

- Uppercuts:
  - Typically reserved for counters
  - Effective when used with three-three setup

- Drill cues & sounds:
  - "boom boom" for jab step outs
  - "pop, pop" for double jab peppering
  - Emphasis on rhythm and timing when faking and finishing
