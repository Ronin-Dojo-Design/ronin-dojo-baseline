For a martial arts program like **Tuff Buffs** at [CU Boulder](https://www.colorado.edu/), the best translation uses Kanji that balance "toughness" (sturdiness/strength) with the "bison" (Buffs) identity in a way that feels authentic to traditional Japanese martial arts.

Recommended Translations

- **剛野牛 (Gō-Yagyū)**: **Sturdy/Tough Bison**
    - **剛 (Gō)**: Means "sturdy," "robust," or "strong". It is the same Kanji used in _Gōjū-ryū_ (Hard-Soft style) karate, making it very appropriate for a martial arts context.
    - **野牛 (Yagyū)**: The formal Kanji for "bison" or "wild ox".
- **強牛 (Kyō-gyū / Tsuyo-ushi)**: **Strong Bull/Ox**
    - **強 (Kyō/Tsuyoi)**: The most common character for "strong" or "powerful".
    - **牛 (Gyū/Ushi)**: The general Kanji for "bison," "cow," or "ox". This is simpler and very common in logos.

Traditional School Naming (for T-shirts)

If you want the name to look like a traditional Japanese _dojo_ or organization, you should add a suffix:

- **剛野牛武術 (Gō-Yagyū Bujutsu)**: Tuff Buffs Martial Arts.
- **剛野牛会 (Gō-Yagyū Kai)**: Tuff Buffs Association (a very common way to name a club or group).

## Important Branding Note

Since you are running a program _at_ CU Boulder, please double-check with the branding office regarding the use of the official "CU" or "Buffalo" logos if you intend to sell these publicly.

- Official Logos: The "Interlocking CU" and "Ralphie" logos generally cannot be modified or combined directly with other graphics (like our samurai).
- Club Usage: Student organizations often have specific "lockup" templates they must use.
- Recommendation: For a custom martial arts design like this, it is safest to keep the artwork separate from the official CU logo (e.g., put the custom art on the back/chest and the official club logo on the sleeve).
